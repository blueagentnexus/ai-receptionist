# AI Receptionist — Executive Summary

**One-page read for Chris. Full detail in `market-landscape.md`.**

## Does the product already exist?

**Not the one you described. Adjacent products do.**

- **Business-side AI receptionists:** 20+ mature players (Marlie.ai, Smith.ai, Dialzara, Goodcall, etc.) — but they target small businesses at $50–$500/mo. Not your market.
- **Consumer call screeners:** Google Pixel Call Screen, iOS 26 Call Screening (shipped 2025), Aura.com Call Assistant, Robokiller, Truecaller, Hiya. All are narrower than your vision.
- **The "personality-driven AI receptionist with agentic follow-through for the average person" — nobody has nailed it. That's the gap.**

## Can you actually build it?

**Yes. Without writing code yourself.**

- Commodity building blocks exist: Twilio (phone numbers) + Retell AI / Vapi (voice agent) + ElevenLabs (voice/personality) + Gemini/Claude (brain) + Tavily (live caller verification).
- Path 1 MVP (cloud receptionist, web dashboard, no mobile app): achievable in 4–8 weeks of focused work, ~$500–$3K prototype budget. I can drive the build.
- Don't start with a native iOS/Android app — save that for after product-market fit.

## Who will you be competing with most directly?

1. **Apple & Google** — they're moving in from the OS side. You must be meaningfully better than iOS 26's built-in call screening.
2. **Aura.com** — the closest direct competitor, but their Call Assistant is a sub-feature of ID-theft protection, not a hero product.
3. **Indie apps** (Safina.ai, AI Call Assistant & Screener) — fragmented, no breakout winner yet.

## Your unfair advantages (to build around)

1. **Personality & voice cloning** — your receptionist sounds like *you*, not a robot.
2. **Live caller verification** — real-time web search to confirm "is ABC Business real?"
3. **Agentic actions** — post to social, send texts, update calendar on command.
4. **Consumer pricing** ($15/mo, not $300).
5. **Senior-care angle** — seniors lose ~$3B/yr to phone scams. Adult children will pay for a solution for mom/dad.

## Biggest risks

- **Apple/Google squeezing from the OS layer** — you have a ~18–36 month window to establish a brand.
- **Call-recording consent laws** (12 two-party states) — solvable with proper disclosures.
- **Telephony workarounds are clunky** — conditional forwarding codes vs. a dedicated number.
